# Financial Metrics Reference

## Valuation Ratios

| Metric | Formula | Interpretation |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| P/E (TTM) | Price / EPS (trailing 12 months) | <15 undervalued, 15-25 fair, >25 growth or overvalued |
| Forward P/E | Price / Forward EPS estimate | Lower than TTM = expected earnings growth |
| PEG | P/E / Annual EPS growth rate | <1 undervalued relative to growth, >2 expensive |
| P/B | Price / Book value per share | <1 trading below asset value, >3 premium |
| P/S | Market cap / Revenue | Sector-dependent, useful for unprofitable companies |
| EV/EBITDA | Enterprise value / EBITDA | <10 often considered cheap, sector-dependent |

## Profitability Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | Sector-dependent, higher = better pricing power |
| Operating Margin | Operating Income / Revenue | >15% generally strong |
| Net Margin | Net Income / Revenue | >10% for most sectors |
| ROE | Net Income / Shareholders' Equity | >15% strong, >20% excellent |
| ROA | Net Income / Total Assets | >5% generally good |
| ROIC | NOPAT / Invested Capital | >WACC means value creation |

## Financial Health Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5-3.0 healthy |
| Quick Ratio | (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities | >1.0 healthy |
| Debt/Equity | Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity | <1.0 conservative, sector-dependent |
| Interest Coverage | EBIT / Interest Expense | >3x comfortable, >5x strong |
| FCF Yield | Free Cash Flow / Market Cap | >5% attractive |

## Cash Flow Quality Signals

- **Operating CF > Net Income**: High-quality earnings (accruals are low)
- **Operating CF < Net Income**: Potential earnings quality issues
- **Capex / Operating CF**: <30% is capital-light, >60% is capital-intensive
- **FCF Trend**: Consistently positive and growing is ideal
- **Stock-Based Compensation**: High SBC dilutes existing shareholders

## Red Flags to Watch

- Declining revenue with increasing inventory
- Rising accounts receivable faster than revenue (collection issues)
- Goodwill significantly larger than tangible assets (acquisition risk)
- Negative operating cash flow with positive net income
- Increasing debt without corresponding asset or revenue growth
- Frequent "one-time" charges that recur every year
